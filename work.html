The Four Pillars of Object-Oriented Programming (OOP): Abstraction, Encapsulation, Inheritance, and Polymorphism.

The essay reflects on the author's journey learning Object-Oriented Programming's four pillars: Abstraction (hiding complex details), Encapsulation (protecting data), Inheritance (reusing code), and Polymorphism (many forms of one action). Initially coding without understanding why methods worked, the author learned through failures that these principles transform code from merely functional to truly well-built, maintainable, and elegant. The key lesson is that while hard work drives progress, these four pillars provide the direction for coding better, not just coding more.
